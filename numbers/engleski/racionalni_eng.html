Rational numbers are a set of numbers that can be expressed in the form of a fraction a/b, where a and b are integers, and b is not equal to zero (b ≠ 0). In other words, rational numbers are all numbers that can be represented as the quotient of two integers. The notation for the set of rational numbers is ℚ. Every whole number is also a rational number because it can be represented as a fraction with a denominator of 1 (e.g., 5 = 5/1). Rational numbers include whole numbers because every whole number can be expressed as a fraction with a denominator of 1. They also encompass numbers with decimal representations that are either finite or have a periodic, repeating pattern. For example: 1. 1/2 is a rational number. 2. 3/4 is a rational number. 3. 5 is a rational number because it can be expressed as 5/1. 4. 0.333... (repeating decimal) is a rational number. Every operation (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with rational numbers results in a rational number, except in cases of division by zero. Rational numbers are important in many mathematical disciplines and have wide applications in practical situations, including finance, engineering, and other fields.
