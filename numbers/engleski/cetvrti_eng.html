Whole numbers are a set of numbers that includes natural numbers, their negatives (opposites), and zero. The notation for the set of whole numbers is â„¤. Therefore, the set of whole numbers encompasses all numbers found on the real number line, both to the right of zero (positive natural numbers) and to the left of zero (negative natural numbers). The set of whole numbers includes: 1. Positive natural numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, ... 2. Negative natural numbers: -1, -2, -3, -4, ... 3. Zero: 0. This set of numbers is used to describe positions on the real line, where positive numbers are located to the right of zero and negative numbers to the left. For example: ... -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ... Whole numbers are often used in mathematical operations and problems involving opposites and relative values. Arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) between whole numbers yield a result that also belongs to the set of whole numbers. Whole numbers serve as the basis for defining other types of numbers, such as rational and real numbers. This class of numbers has wide applications in various mathematical and everyday situations.
